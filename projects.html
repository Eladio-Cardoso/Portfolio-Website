Undisclosed Fantasy Novel



The winter was bitter, bitterer than raw nettle salad and unfermented swamp grog, bitterer than the thickest bark brew, bitterer than a mother you never knew.

We marched on snow, crunching through it on leather bound and blistered feet, the howl of our stomachs matching the winds. Nabu followed sullenly behind his father, mindlessly abandoned to fantasy, gazing at the foot and wheel trodden snow, mashed with dirt in layers beneath his feet. It reminded us of the striped white and black gneiss rock floor and walls of our homeland, Starfall.

It was a small settlement for dwarven standards, big enough however, to house thousands of people underground. The local human community knew us as the dwarves of HoneyHill, who lived inside a hill and tended to the beehives outside of it. Further settlements called us the Anthill dwellers, sometimes in a derogatory way. To ourselves we kept the name Starfall, that was our ancestral name, whereupon eons ago a star had struck the hill and gifted us the wisdom of the silver iron.

Three whole seasons had come and gone since the caravan had set out from there, it was now a livelier memory than ever, fading however, behind leagues of forested hills and rocky mountainous terrain.

"Nabu!" - spoke Nabu's father, Uriel - "Go see with the drive master if the donkeys need a rest, they are stubborn even for their duty."

The expedition's sole remaining carriage, was a wooden two wheeler pulled by a pair of donkeys and loaded with all the remaining food. 8 great wheels of cheese, 12 bags of barley flour, 4 crates of biscuits and 3 big deerskins with olive oil.

Before departing Starfall, Uriel had been zealous about the preparation of the caravan's supplies. "Bind the cheese in cloth, dip them in olive oil, smear them in clay and let them dry. Double bag the floor sacks, smear them with lard and put them in a crate. I want the biscuits hard enough to hammer a nail and the olive oil skins soft as cloaks, also inside crates, I want the crates well tied in rope. If anything falls off a mountain it should break the floor before itself."

"Yes father" spoke Nabu, waking from his visions of home as he hurried towards the front of the convoy.

At the vanguard of the expedition line, along with the donkey pulled carriage, marched several iron mailed dwarves, heads covered in linen, wool and round iron plate helms, with long hexagonal wooden shields strapped on their backs, iron spears in hand and steel axepicks strung on their belts. Every one of them wore a linen tabard over armor with a paiting of a stonewall on the chest. This was the stonewall company, lead by Arrié the unbroken. One of the things they were always responsible to carry with them at all times was a locking torc, a curled iron hoop with an open side meant to lock their long diamond shaped shields together during battle. One of these dwarves had lost it, and with it, all respect Arrié had for him.

As Nabu arrived at the carriage he heard Arrie’s voice growing in ire “I would have said that you haven’t lost your head yet because it’s attached to your neck, but it seems to me YOU CLEARLY LOST YOUR HEAD SON!” exploded Arrié “What use are you as a shield dwarf now without the torc? Off to the sidelines until you find it. . . ” he continued, now in a resigned tone. The other expedition members, despite being sad for the poor dwarf, held a smile at the vigorous outbreak of Arrié for it had lifted the spirits of the expedition members by knowing one of them still had such energy in the face of such bleak ordeal. Nabu held a snicker of his own, and wished Nissi had been here to witness the scene
